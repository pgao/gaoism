N-TROPY

120 lb semi-autonomous robot built for FIRST Robotics competition. Features a dextrous timing-belt driven telescoping arm used to pick and place inner tubes onto 10 foot tall pegs and a novel minibot that utilises neodymium magnets to climb a 6-foot tall steel pole. Won the BAE Systems Design Award for a uniquely modular electronics system: the "E-Box" and landed our team as quarterfinalists in the San Diego Robotics Regional Competition. I served as the executive president that year, in charge of the overall design and construction of the robot, in addition to managing the logistical and organisational requirements of a 100-person team.

Chrysanthemums

Project examining the antimicrobial properties of multiple species of chrysanthemums. I was inspired to do this project after catching the flu and being given copious amounts of chrysanthemum tea by my dad. I wondered whether its disease-fighting properties were worth drinking 50 damned cups of the stuff a day. I tested chrysanthemum's effect on gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria as compared to conventional ampecillin antibiotics. Unfortunately, while chrysanthemum does help stave off bacteria a bit, you're probably better off chugging pills for now. Won 2nd place in the Microbiology category at the Synopsys Science Fair.

pharad

120 lb semi-autonomous robot built for FIRST Robotics competition. Essentially used to play soccer. Features include a custom-machined, tracked drive train capable of traversing extremely uneven terrain at high speed with no damage, utilising a pneumatically driven kicker to play the robot equivalent of soccer. I served as the co-technical president, overseeing systems integration and doing all the computer-aided-design work for digitally constructing the robot before physically fabricating it.
